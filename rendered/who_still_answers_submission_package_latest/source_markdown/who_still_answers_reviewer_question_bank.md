# Reviewer Question Bank: Option C

Project: `Public Answer-Supply Reallocation in the Generative-AI Period: Evidence from Stack Overflow`  
Date: April 4, 2026

## 1. What is the paper about in one sentence?

The paper studies whether, in more AI-substitutable Stack Overflow domains, the public answer queue became more dependent on recent entrants in earlier and endorsed answer roles, whether those entrants appear less durable under bounded inference, and whether that reallocation is associated with weaker downstream public certification.

## 2. Is this a direct AI-use paper?

No. It is a bounded differential-exposure paper using pre-shock AI substitutability as the key comparison.

## 3. Is this a clean ChatGPT shock paper?

No. The timing diagnostics are too mixed for that. The correct language is `ChatGPT-period differential exposure`.

## 4. What are the three promoted evidence families?

1. durability of newly observed public answerers  
2. answer-role reallocation toward early and endorsed roles  
3. an infrastructure bridge from role gaps to slower endorsed resolution and weaker certification

## 5. Which family is strongest after WS6?

The bridge family is strongest under the conservative inferential layer, the answer-role family is next, and durability is promoted but more bounded.

## 6. What is the safest durability claim?

That the post-transition public entrant margin appears less durable in more exposed domains under clustered and `CR2` inference, with weaker support under the strictest resampling checks.

## 7. Does the paper claim accepted-current roles do not move?

No. It claims the entrant-heavy shift is weaker in `accepted_current` roles than in `first_positive` and `top_score` roles.

## 8. Does the paper claim mediation?

No. The infrastructure bridge is written as an associated downstream certification consequence, not a mediation theorem.

## 9. Why is this an IS contribution rather than a niche platform paper?

Because the contribution is about how private AI can reconfigure the public labor that sustains a reusable knowledge infrastructure. Contributor composition is treated as a first-order platform state variable, not a decorative heterogeneity cut.

## 10. What is the main identification boundary?

The paper does not observe direct AI use and does not isolate a clean release-date treatment effect.

## 11. What is the current outlet recommendation?

`JMIS` is the safer primary target in the current manuscript form; `ISR` remains plausible only after a full article-scale manuscript rebuild.

## 12. What is the biggest remaining blocker?

The main blocker is no longer missing mechanism. It is journal-scale completeness: literature, theory, methods exposition, and results architecture still need to be expanded for a serious FT50 submission.
