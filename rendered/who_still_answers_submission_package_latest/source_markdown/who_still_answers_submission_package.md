## Current Safe Claim

`In the 2020-2025 Stack Overflow archive, more AI-substitutable domains show a bounded mechanism stack in which the surviving public queue becomes more residualized, incumbent answer capacity thins, recent entrants become more visible in earlier and endorsed answer roles than in accepted-current certification, and larger early-versus-accepted role gaps are associated with weaker downstream public certification. The strongest consequence evidence is the certification layer, but the paper's identity is the queue -> capacity/role -> certification stack rather than a bridge-only story. Newly observed public answerers also appear less durable under clustered and CR2 inference, though more bounded under the strictest resampling checks. The timing story is reinforced by an external AI-salience layer, a same-setting internal AI-title trace, and one strict question-side AI-ban timing layer, which is the package's only canonical restricted timing check. The exposure story is reinforced by question-level residualization, a broader thread-level disclosed-AI corpus, a strict question-side disclosed-AI subset used only for the canonical restricted timing check, a transparent 16-tag exposure ranking, and JetBrains developer-survey calibration at the matched-cluster level. `AIDev` remains the strongest certification-focused direct-AI-use external pillar through domain-overlap and fix-like subsets, while `DevGPT` is optional question-like support rather than a coequal pillar.`

The package must also keep the following non-negotiable boundaries:

- no claim of complete direct observation of AI use in the main Stack Overflow setting
- no pristine release-date treatment story
- no claim that accepted-current roles do not move at all
- no claim that brand-new platform users flooded the answer pipeline
- no mediation claim for the bridge
- no generic platform-collapse claim
- no claim that current Unix or Super User second-setting runs independently replicate the full main-setting mechanism stack
- no claim that `DevGPT` alone rescues the second-pillar problem
- no claim that `AIDev` is a one-for-one replication of Stack Overflow
- no claim that the pooled cross-setting panel rescues the main identification design on its own
- no claim that moderation / closure mining is a promoted empirical pillar rather than appendix-only context
- no reviewer-facing use of contributor fixed-effects around first disclosure
