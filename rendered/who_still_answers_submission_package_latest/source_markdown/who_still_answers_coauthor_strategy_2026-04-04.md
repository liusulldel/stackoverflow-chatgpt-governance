## Bottom line

Yes, interest from the right IS coauthor would be a meaningful signal, but it is **not** the next highest-leverage move if the goal is to maximize ISR odds. The right coauthor can:

- sharpen theorem and journal positioning
- pressure-test what ISR reviewers will attack first
- improve the paper's legitimacy and editorial readability

The right coauthor cannot:

- create direct AI-use measurement in the main setting
- convert bounded timing into a clean discontinuity
- make a weak second setting become strong by reputation alone

So coauthor outreach becomes genuinely valuable only when the paper is `coauthor-sent ready`, not while the core data path is still obviously under construction.

## Is interest from them a good signal?

Yes, but only under the right interpretation.

### Good signal

It is a strong signal if:

- they engage with the theorem and mechanism rather than just saying the topic is interesting
- they do not immediately ask for a totally different paper identity
- they respond that the paper is close enough to be worth serious revision time

That would mean the paper is crossing from `interesting project` into `serious field-legible contribution`.

### Weak signal

It is only a weak signal if:

- they offer light comments but do not want their name on it
- they like the topic but say it still needs more evidence first
- they suggest a safer outlet immediately

That would still be useful feedback, but not a true positive quality signal.

## Coauthor-sent ready: exact threshold

### Not ready to send yet

Do **not** send broad coauthor outreach while these are still true:

- the paper still lacks the next same-setting disclosed-AI corpus layer
- the restricted AI-ban timing design has not been attempted
- the paper cannot explain in two sentences why `AIDev` is useful but not overclaimed
- the manuscript still reads like multiple related findings instead of one theorem

### Ready to send

The paper is `coauthor-sent ready` once all of the following are true:

1. one stable theorem sentence controls the abstract and introduction
2. the same-setting disclosed-AI corpus exists beyond titles
3. the AI-ban restricted timing prototype has been run and written up honestly
4. `AIDev` / `DevGPT` positioning is fixed as bounded external validation rather than coequal replication
5. the package includes:
   - main manuscript
   - 1-page evidence summary
   - 1-page ask for the prospective coauthor
   - explicit list of what is still structurally weak

That is the point where outreach becomes efficient rather than premature.

## Recommended outreach style

Do not send the whole package cold with a vague ask.

Best outreach packet:

1. one short email
2. one-page summary with:
   - theorem
   - why the paper matters for ISR
   - current strongest evidence
   - remaining structural weakness
   - exact help requested
3. full manuscript only as attachment or follow-up

## Final recommendation

If the goal is highest ISR upside, the order should be:

1. finish the next data round
2. then send targeted coauthor outreach
3. then do final ISR polish with that person's input

So:

`coauthor outreach is valuable, but it is a round-after-next move, not the immediate next move.`
